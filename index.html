The Artist Phase (Ages 0-5)

Theme: Embrace Their Physical Needs

Key Verse: "The Lord is your keeper; the Lord is your shade on your right hand." (Psalm 121:5)

Goal for Parents

To teach parents that at this stage, "Love God" is taught through "Love Me." Because they are motivated by safety, their view of God is shaped by how safe they feel with their primary caregivers.

The 3 Big Questions to Answer

  • "Am I safe?" Answered by providing consistent routines (bedtime, mealtimes).
  • "Am I able?" Answered by patient encouragement as they learn new basic skills.
  • "Am I okay?" Answered by giving them comfort when they fail, get hurt, or feel scared.

Take-Home Action: The Bedtime Blessing

Speak a 2-sentence blessing over your child tonight. Example: "[Name], you are safe because God loves you, and I love you. You are a gift from Him."

The Scientist Phase (Ages 6-10)

Theme: Engage Their Interests

Key Verse: "Great are the works of the Lord, studied by all who delight in them." (Psalm 111:2)

Goal for Parents

To encourage children to trust God's character. At this stage, faith moves from just "feeling" to "logic." They want to know why we believe what we believe.

The 3 Big Questions to Answer

  • "Do I have your attention?" Showing them they matter more than our phones.
  • "Do I have what it takes?" Helping them find their early skills and gifts.
  • "Do I have friends?" Supporting their growing social circles at church and school.

Take-Home Action: The Saturday Scientist

Pick one thing your child is interested in this weekend (Lego, a sport, drawing) and engage with it together. Ask them: "I wonder how God thought of this?"

The Engineer Phase (Ages 11-13)

Theme: Affirm Their Personal Journey

Key Verse: "I praise you, for I am fearfully and wonderfully made. Wonderful are your works; my soul knows it very well." (Psalm 139:14)

Goal for Parents

Middle schoolers are "engineering" their identity. Everything feels under construction. Your job is to shift from "fixing" their problems to "affirming" their worth in Christ.

The 2 Big Questions to Answer

  • "Who do I like?" Navigating shifting friend groups and peer pressure.
  • "Who am I?" Helping them base their worth on God's design, not online comparison or performance.

Take-Home Action: The Affirmation Note

Write a text message or a sticky note to your teen that says: "I saw [positive character trait] in you today. It reminds me of how God made you." Focus on who they are, not what they achieved.

The Philosopher Phase (Ages 14-18)

Theme: Mobilize Their Potential

Key Verse: "Look carefully then how you walk, not as unwise but as wise, making the best use of the time." (Ephesians 5:15-16)

Goal for Parents

To help them move from a borrowed faith (yours) to an owned faith (theirs). You must transition into a "Consultant" role—offering wisdom when asked, allowing them to lead.

The 4 Big Questions to Answer

  • "Where do I belong?" Finding healthy community outside the home.
  • "Why should I believe?" Addressing their natural doubts with patience, not panic.
  • "How can I matter?" Showing them how to use their gifts right now.
  • "What will I do?" Preparing them for work, college, and adulthood.

Take-Home Action: Ask, Don't Tell

When your teen has a problem this week, instead of giving the answer, try asking: "I'm curious, what led you to that conclusion?" or "How can I best support you in this decision?"

Family Tech Covenant

Family Tech Covenant

"All things are lawful for me, but I will not be dominated by anything." - 1 Corinthians 6:12

We believe that technology is a tool given to us to create, connect, and learn. However, we also know it can be a distraction from what matters most: loving God and loving each other. We agree to the following commitments:

1. We will protect our time and rest.

We agree that sleep is a gift from God. Therefore, all personal screens will be turned off and plugged into the family charging station by: PM.

2. We will prioritize people over screens.

When we are eating meals together, riding in the car as a family, or someone is talking directly to us, we will put our devices down and give our full attention.

3. We will act with honesty and integrity.

We will not hide apps, use secret accounts, or delete browser history. Parents promise to review devices as mentors who want to help, not just as police looking for a crime.

4. We will ask for help when we mess up.

If we see something online that makes us uncomfortable, scared, or tempted, we promise to tell a parent or trusted adult immediately. Parents promise to stay calm and help, without immediately threatening to take the device away forever.
